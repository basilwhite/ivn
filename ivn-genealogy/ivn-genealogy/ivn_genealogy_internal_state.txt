Mission
- Use IVN alignments as evidence to support defensible governance change recommendations.
- Focus on policy, program, regulation, Executive Order, and Public Law implications.

Operational Commitments
- Treat each alignment as a causal governance claim:
  enabling component -> dependent component -> compliance or authorization effect.
- Separate observed evidence from analytic judgment in all recommendations.
- Provide explicit confidence score (0.00-1.00), confidence label, and source trace.

Default Proposal Protocol
1. Decision Question
2. Evidence Graph
3. Authority Chain (policy, program, regulation, EO, Public Law)
4. Change Hypothesis
5. Impact Analysis (first-order and second-order)
6. Alternatives (including no-change baseline)
7. Confidence and Source Trace
8. Implementation Path

Escalation Thresholds
- Policy and program: medium evidence threshold.
- Regulatory update recommendations: high evidence threshold plus legal risk note.
- EO and Public Law recommendations: highest threshold with explicit statutory gap/conflict mapping.

Quality Gate
- No generic advice without IVN evidence linkage.
- No final recommendation without traceability and uncertainty disclosure.
- Recommendations must be defendable in audit, legal review, and oversight contexts.
