# Strategic Alignment Report: M-25-22 and IVN

**Date:** April 28, 2026

## 1. Executive Summary

This report details the strategic alignment between OMB Memorandum M-25-22, "Driving Efficient Acquisition of Artificial Intelligence in Government," and the USDA's Internal Vision for the Nation (IVN) database. Our analysis, using a keyword-based scoring model, reveals significant synergies, particularly concerning **risk management, compliance, and system authorization**.

The key takeaway is that USDA's existing governance frameworks and data sources, as cataloged in the IVN, provide a strong foundation for meeting the AI acquisition requirements mandated by M-25-22. By leveraging these existing assets, USDA can ensure a compliant, secure, and efficient AI adoption process.

This document groups the most significant alignments by their relevant M-25-22 components and concludes with a consolidated set of actionable recommendations for leadership to capitalize on these findings.

## 3. Consolidated Recommendations

To leverage these strategic alignments, we propose the following actionable recommendations for leadership:

| Recommendation ID | Recommendation | M-25-22 Component(s) Addressed |
|:---|:---|:---|
| REC-01 | **Integrate AI Risk into ERM:** Formally integrate the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0) into the agency's existing Enterprise Risk Management (ERM) processes as defined by OMB Circular A-123. | 4.d.iii.D |
| REC-02 | **Update ATO Procedures for AI:** Direct the OCIO to update the agency's Authorization to Operate (ATO) procedures to include specific controls and considerations for AI systems, referencing NIST SP 800-37 and SP 800-53. | 4.e.i |
| REC-03 | **Develop AI-Specific Contract Language:** Create standardized contract clauses for AI acquisitions that explicitly require compliance with M-25-21 and reference the risk management practices found in the aligned OMB memoranda. | 4.d.iii.D |
| REC-04 | **Mandate Cross-Functional Teams:** For all AI procurements, mandate the formation of cross-functional teams including representatives from acquisition, legal, privacy, and cybersecurity to ensure all facets of risk and compliance are addressed from the outset. | 3.b, 4.a.i |
