## 1. Executive Summary

This report provides an analysis of the Office of Management and Budget (OMB) Memorandum M-25-22, "Driving Efficient Acquisition of Artificial Intelligence in Government," and its alignment with the resources currently cataloged in the Information Vehicle aNd database (IVN). The analysis reveals several key areas where existing guidance and frameworks within the IVN can be leveraged to support compliance with M-25-22.

This report offers specific recommendations for managing and communicating these alignments to ensure the Department is well-positioned to acquire and implement AI technologies responsibly, efficiently, and in full compliance with federal mandates.

## 2. Background

M-25-22 establishes critical requirements for Federal agencies to ensure the responsible acquisition and use of Artificial Intelligence. The memorandum is grounded in three core themes:
*   Ensuring a competitive AI marketplace to benefit the government and the public.
*   Safeguarding taxpayer dollars by tracking AI performance and managing risk.
*   Promoting effective AI acquisition through cross-functional engagement.

The IVN database serves as a repository of key documents, policies, and guidance relevant to the Department's operations. By crosswalking M-25-22 with the IVN, we can identify existing resources that support compliance and pinpoint areas where new guidance may be needed.

## 3. Discovered Alignments

Our analysis identified several significant alignments between the components of M-25-22 and documents within the IVN database. These alignments provide a foundation for developing a comprehensive compliance strategy.

### 3.1. Risk Management and Performance Tracking

**M-25-22 Component:** "Track AI performance and manage risk."

**IVN Alignments:**
*   **NIST AI 100-1, Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0):** This is a cornerstone document for AI risk management.
*   **OMB Memorandum M-16-17, Management's Responsibility for Enterprise Risk Management and Internal Control:** Provides a broad framework for risk management that can be adapted for AI.
*   **USDA 2024 Annual Performance Plan and Report:** Demonstrates existing processes for performance tracking that can be extended to AI systems.

### 3.2. Cross-Functional Engagement

**M-25-22 Component:** "Foster cross-functional engagement (acquisition, IT, cybersecurity, privacy, civil rights, etc.)."

**IVN Alignments:**
*   **OMB M-16-04, Cybersecurity Strategy and Implementation Plan (CSIP):** Provides a model for inter-agency and cross-functional collaboration.
*   **OMB M-21-02, Guidance on Federal Information Security and Privacy Management Requirements:** Highlights the intersection of security and privacy, requiring collaboration between these teams.
*   **USDA Office of Civil Rights - Budget Explanatory Notes 2025:** Indicates the established role of the Office of Civil Rights, a key stakeholder in AI acquisition.

### 3.3. Requirements and Acquisition

**M-25-22 Component:** "Communicate clear and specific requirements."

**IVN Alignments:**
*   **OMB M-21-02, Guidance on Federal Information Security and Privacy Management Requirements:** Provides a template for articulating clear and specific requirements in the context of security and privacy.
*   **Departmental Manual on Cybersecurity Incident Management:** An example of a detailed guidance document that establishes clear procedures and requirements.

## 4. Recommendations for Leadership

Based on the alignments discovered, we recommend the following actions to ensure and demonstrate compliance with M-25-22:

### 4.1. Management and Governance

1.  **Adopt and Mandate the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (RMF):**
    *   **Action:** Formally adopt the NIST AI RMF as the Department's standard for managing risks associated with AI systems.
    *   **Rationale:** The AI RMF is directly aligned with M-25-22's emphasis on risk management and is already cataloged in the IVN. Mandating its use will provide a consistent and repeatable process for AI risk assessment.

2.  **Extend Performance Tracking to AI Systems:**
    *   **Action:** Update the Department's performance management guidance to include specific metrics for AI systems, leveraging the framework in the `USDA 2024 Annual Performance Plan and Report`.
    *   **Rationale:** M-25-22 requires tracking AI performance. Integrating AI metrics into existing performance management processes is more efficient than creating a new, separate system.

3.  **Establish a Cross-Functional AI Acquisition Working Group:**
    *   **Action:** Create a formal working group for AI acquisition that includes representatives from acquisition, IT, cybersecurity, privacy, civil rights, and legal, modeled after the collaborative structures suggested in documents like the `Cybersecurity Strategy and Implementation Plan (CSIP)`.
    *   **Rationale:** M-25-22 mandates cross-functional engagement. A dedicated working group will ensure all relevant perspectives are included in the AI acquisition process from the outset.

### 4.2. Communication and Demonstration of Compliance

1.  **Develop an "M-25-22 Compliance Crosswalk" Document:**
    *   **Action:** Create a formal document that explicitly maps the requirements of M-25-22 to the specific policies and guidance documents in the IVN database.
    *   **Rationale:** This document will serve as a ready reference for auditors and oversight bodies, demonstrating that the Department has a clear understanding of its compliance obligations and the resources it is using to meet them.

2.  **Incorporate AI into Existing Training and Awareness Programs:**
    *   **Action:** Update existing training materials on topics like privacy, cybersecurity, and acquisition to include modules on AI-specific considerations, drawing from the aligned documents in the IVN.
    *   **Rationale:** This approach is more efficient than creating a standalone AI training program and ensures that AI is not treated as a siloed issue.

3.  **Highlight AI Governance in External Communications:**
    *   **Action:** When communicating with the public and other agencies, proactively highlight the Department's use of established frameworks like the NIST AI RMF to govern its use of AI.
    *   **Rationale:** This will build public trust and demonstrate the Department's commitment to the responsible and ethical use of AI, in line with the principles of M-25-22.

## 5. Conclusion

M-25-22 presents a significant opportunity to strengthen the Department's approach to AI acquisition and governance. By leveraging the existing resources within the IVN database and implementing the recommendations in this report, the Department can ensure it is not only compliant with federal mandates but also a leader in the responsible adoption of Artificial Intelligence.
